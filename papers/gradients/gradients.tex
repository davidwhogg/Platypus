\begin{abstract}
% Context:
The \apogee\ infrared spectroscopic survey and \thecannon\ model of
stellar spectra have together produced precise abundances for 15
elements for $10^5$ red-giant stars throughout the Milky Way disk.
These data make possible extremely precise measurements of chemical
abundance differences between different components of the Milky Way.
% Aims:
We visualize and measure the 15-dimensional chemical-abundance distribution of
red-giant stars as a function of galactocentric radius, near the plane
of the disk, to determine the chemical-abundance gradients, but also
test ideas of star formation, Galactic nucleosynthesis, and radial
migration.
% Methods:
Red-giant distances are determined HOW?
Chemical-abundance gradients---in mean abundance and abundance
dispersion---are determined by linear regression.
% Results:
We find STUFF.
\end{abstract}
